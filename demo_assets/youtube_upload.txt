DESCRIPTION
============================================================
I gave Filmwriter one sentence. Twenty-four minutes later it handed me a finished two-minute film — no shot list, no images, no edits.

Filmwriter is an autonomous AI showrunner built on Qwen Cloud. You type one logline; a crew of eight Qwen-powered agents writes it, storyboards it, shoots it, scores it, checks its own frames, and cuts the final film — with no human in the loop. When a shot breaks continuity, drifts on motion, or trips a legal/clearance check, the crew re-shoots it until it clears QC, then self-grades the result. Finished films bank their cast in a "season vault," so the same characters walk back on set for the next episode.

It's honestly not fully baked — the hardest shots still slip — but that's the whole thesis: every pass makes the showrunner better, and it grades its own progress.

HOW IT WORKS
• A crew of 8 agents: Showrunner, Screenwriter, Story Editor, Storyboard, Continuity, Legal & Clearances, Cinematographer, Editor
• Self-correcting: each department fixes the exact violation and re-audits — seven runs took continuity breaks from 9 down to 3
• A Legal & Clearances agent screens every frame for trademarks, logos, and legible on-screen text
• Season vault → recurring cast; one-off clips become episodes
• Every finished film grades itself: continuity, identity, beats, through-line, craft
• Runs on nine models on Qwen Cloud / Alibaba Cloud (qwen-max, qwen-plus, qwen3-vl, qwen-image, qwen-image-edit, wan2.6, wan2.2-kf2v, qwen3-tts, qwen3-asr)

The honest tradeoff: every fix is another model call, so quality has a price — balancing quality against cost is the real engineering problem.
