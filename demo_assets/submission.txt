PROJECT NAME
============================================================
Filmwriter

============================================================
TAGLINE (elevator pitch)
============================================================
An autonomous AI showrunner on Qwen Cloud — one sentence in, a full film out.

============================================================
WHAT IT DOES
============================================================
Filmwriter turns a single logline into a finished short film — written, storyboarded, shot, scored, checked, and cut entirely by a crew of eight Qwen-powered agents, with no human in the loop. When a frame breaks continuity, drifts on motion, or trips a legal/clearance check, the crew re-shoots it until it clears quality control, then the film grades itself. Finished films bank their cast in a "season vault," so the same characters return for the next episode — clips become a series.

============================================================
INSPIRATION
============================================================
Every text-to-video demo stops at a single clip. But a film is thousands of decisions: how many scenes, long take or montage, does your lead still look like your lead in shot seven. We wanted to know whether a team of AI agents could make all of those calls and correct their own mistakes — producing not just a clip, but a directed film, and a season.

============================================================
HOW WE BUILT IT
============================================================
• Nine models on Qwen Cloud / Alibaba Cloud (DashScope): qwen-max and qwen-plus (planning + writing), qwen3-vl (visual QA + legal/clearance review), qwen-image and qwen-image-edit (stills), wan2.6 and wan2.2-kf2v (image-to-video), qwen3-tts (narration), qwen3-asr (read-back verification).
• A crew of 8 agents — Showrunner, Screenwriter, Story Editor, Storyboard, Continuity, Legal & Clearances, Cinematographer, Editor — orchestrated by a conductor.
• A self-correction loop: each department inspects its own output, flags the exact violation, and re-rolls or re-shoots until it passes review; streaming animation overlaps QA with rendering.
• A season vault that banks the audited character sheet + locked wardrobe so episodes reuse the same cast.
• Self-grading: every film scores itself on continuity, identity, beats, through-line, and craft — from the crew's real QA signals, not guesswork.
• Node.js backend on Alibaba Cloud ECS, served over a Cloudflare tunnel, with a live web app.

============================================================
CHALLENGES WE RAN INTO
============================================================
• Continuity is the hard problem. Reference anchoring hit diminishing returns; the streaming re-roll is the real workhorse. Over seven runs of the same logline, continuity breaks fell from nine to three.
• The quality-vs-cost tradeoff: every correction is another model call, so more iteration means a better film but a bigger bill. Balancing them is the real engineering problem.
• Iteration isn't a straight line — not every loop improved things; some performed worse.
• Keeping on-screen text legible and avoiding IP infringement required a dedicated Legal & Clearances agent.

============================================================
ACCOMPLISHMENTS
============================================================
• A genuinely autonomous pipeline: one sentence to a finished, self-graded film — and a series.
• A self-correcting crew that catches and fixes its own mistakes and knows its own score.
• Honest engineering — we can point to exactly where it still slips and why.

============================================================
WHAT WE LEARNED
============================================================
• Single figure + fixed wardrobe + few scenes is the best case; flowing wardrobe + many cuts is the worst.
• Self-correction beats brute-force reference anchoring.
• The economics (cost per model call) are as much the problem as the quality.

============================================================
WHAT'S NEXT
============================================================
• Close the gap on the hardest continuity shots.
• Richer episodic seasons and animated, living agent cards.
• Drive down cost-per-film.
